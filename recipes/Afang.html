Afang Soup Recipe

Description

Afang soup is a popular Nigerian delicacy originating from the southern part of the country, especially among the Efik and Ibibio people. It is known for its rich flavour, nutritional value, and thick consistency, often served with swallow such as garri or fufu.

Ingredients

  • Palm oil
  • Ground crayfish
  • Fresh or dried fish
  • Cow skin (kpomo)
  • Periwinkle
  • Onions
  • Fresh pepper
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes (Maggi)
  • Afang leaves
  • Water or meat stock

Steps

  1. Wash and cook the meat and cow skin until tender, then set aside.
  2. Heat palm oil in a pot and add chopped onions.
  3. Add the cooked meat, fish, and periwinkle, then stir well.
  4. Add ground crayfish, pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt to taste.
  5. Pour in meat stock or water and allow to simmer.
  6. Add the afang leaves and stir thoroughly.
  7. Allow the soup to cook for a few minutes, then remove from heat.
